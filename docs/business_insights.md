# 📊 Business Insights Report
## Experiment-Driven Pricing Analytics for E-Commerce Growth

---

## 🎯 Objective
The goal of this analysis was to evaluate whether a new pricing strategy (Variant A) leads to improved business performance compared to the existing strategy (Control), primarily focusing on:

- Average Order Value (AOV)
- Revenue trends
- Category-level performance
- Statistical significance of results

---

## 📌 Key Findings

### 1. A/B Test Performance (Overall)
- Variant A shows a **slight increase in average order value** compared to Control.
- However, statistical testing indicates:
  - ❌ No statistically significant difference
  - ❌ Very small effect size
- This suggests that the observed improvement may be due to randomness rather than a true impact of pricing.

👉 **Conclusion:**  
The new pricing strategy does not provide strong enough evidence for a full rollout.

---

### 2. Distribution Analysis (Boxplot Insight)
- Both Control and Variant A show **similar median order values**
- The spread (variance) is also very similar
- Presence of outliers in both variants indicates:
  - High-value purchases exist but are not influenced strongly by pricing change

👉 **Insight:**  
Pricing strategy does not significantly shift customer spending behavior.

---

### 3. Category-Level Performance (Critical Insight)
From the category-wise A/B comparison:

#### Positive Impact Categories:
- Electronics → Strong uplift in Variant A
- Home Decor → Noticeable improvement
- Beauty → Moderate increase

#### Negative / Neutral Categories:
- Grocery → Slight decline
- Sports → Drop in performance
- Books & Toys → Minimal or no change

👉 **Insight:**  
Pricing strategy impact is **not uniform across categories**

👉 **Business Meaning:**  
Certain product categories are more price-sensitive than others.

---

### 4. Revenue Trend Over Time
- Revenue trends for Control and Variant A **overlap heavily**
- No consistent upward pattern for Variant A
- Some spikes exist but are **not sustained over time**

👉 **Insight:**  
- Variant A does not show consistent improvement
- Performance appears volatile and inconsistent

---

## ⚠️ Key Limitations
- Dataset size is relatively small (~1200 records)
- Experiment duration may be insufficient
- External factors (seasonality, region behavior) not controlled
- High variability in order values reduces statistical clarity

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

### 🚫 1. Do NOT Fully Rollout Variant A
- Lack of statistical significance
- No consistent performance improvement

---

### ✅ 2. Apply Targeted Pricing Strategy
Instead of full rollout:
- Focus on high-performing categories:
  - Electronics
  - Home Decor
  - Beauty

👉 Run **category-specific pricing experiments**

---

### 🔁 3. Extend the Experiment
- Increase sample size
- Run experiment for longer duration
- Collect more stable data

---

### 📊 4. Segment-Based Strategy (Next Level)
Future experiments should include:
- Country-wise segmentation
- Customer-type segmentation (new vs returning)
- Price sensitivity analysis

---

## 🧠 Final Conclusion

While Variant A shows slight improvements in some areas, the overall results are:

- ❌ Not statistically significant  
- ❌ Not consistent across time  
- ❌ Not uniform across categories  

👉 Therefore, a **full-scale rollout is not recommended at this stage**

However, **targeted experimentation and deeper segmentation** can unlock potential gains.
